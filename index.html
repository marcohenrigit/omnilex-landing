01 The challenge

A legal function doing high-value work by the hour, by hand.

The client's in-house team was the bottleneck for the whole business. Routine but consequential matters — contract reviews, corporate questions, employment issues — piled up behind a small number of qualified lawyers.

Anything urgent or specialised went to external counsel at premium rates and multi-day turnaround. The work was excellent, but it was slow, expensive, and impossible to scale with demand.

03 The business case

Law-firm quality, at software economics.

The saving is not a discount — it is a different cost structure. The Mind does the labour; our lawyers supervise at leverage; nothing about the quality gives.

01

The Mind does the labour

Research and drafting that once consumed billable hours are done by the Mind in minutes, at the marginal cost of software.

02

Lawyers supervise at leverage

Counsel review and approve rather than execute, so one lawyer stands behind many matters instead of one at a time.

03

No quality trade-off

Every output is grounded in primary law, cited to source, and reviewed before delivery — so speed never costs defensibility.
